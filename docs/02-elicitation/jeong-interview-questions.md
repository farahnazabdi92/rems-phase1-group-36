# REMS Project – Interview Questions Document

## 1. Selected Stakeholder
**Administrative Staff**

### Justification
Administrative staff play a central role in managing the ethics application process. They oversee application completeness, workflow coordination, and communication between stakeholders.  
Their position allows them to provide insights into system inefficiencies, timelines, and process structure, making them a suitable stakeholder for eliciting requirements.

---

## 2. Interview Goal

To understand how administrative staff currently manage ethics applications, identify inefficiencies in workflow and communication, and clarify uncertainties in order to derive system requirements.

---

## 3. Related User Stories

1. **As an administrative staff member, I want to check submitted applications for completeness so that incomplete applications can be identified early.**

2. **As an administrative staff member, I want to track the progress of applications so that I can manage workflow efficiently.**

3. **As an administrative staff member, I want to manage communication between researchers and reviewers so that the review process runs smoothly.**

---

## 4. Interview Questions

### Q1. Checking Completeness of Applications

**Question:**  
How do you currently check whether submitted applications are complete?

**Uncertainty:**  
The process for verifying completeness and identifying missing information is unclear.

**Impact on Requirements:**  
- The system shall support validation of required fields and documents.  
- The system shall highlight incomplete applications.

---

### Q2. Required Information and Documents

**Question:**  
What information and documents are typically required, and do these vary depending on the type of research?

**Uncertainty:**  
It is unclear whether requirements are fixed or vary based on context.

**Impact on Requirements:**  
- The system shall enforce mandatory fields during submission.  
- The system shall support dynamic document requirements.

---

### Q3. Application Workflow

**Question:**  
What steps do applications go through after submission?

**Uncertainty:**  
The workflow and sequence of stages are not clearly defined.

**Impact on Requirements:**  
- The system shall support a structured workflow.  
- The system shall define and track stages of the review process.

---

### Q4. Tracking Application Status

**Question:**  
How do you currently track the status of applications?

**Uncertainty:**  
The method for monitoring progress and identifying delays is unclear.

**Impact on Requirements:**  
- The system shall provide real-time status tracking.  
- The system shall allow monitoring across applications.

---

### Q5. Communication Management

**Question:**  
How do you currently manage communication between researchers and committee members?

**Uncertainty:**  
The communication process and record-keeping methods are unclear.

**Impact on Requirements:**  
- The system shall support stakeholder communication.  
- The system shall maintain communication records.

---

### Q6. Pain Points and Inefficiencies

**Question:**  
What are the most time-consuming or difficult parts of managing applications?

**Uncertainty:**  
Key inefficiencies and bottlenecks are not fully understood.

**Impact on Requirements:**  
- The system shall reduce manual processes.  
- The system shall improve workflow efficiency.
