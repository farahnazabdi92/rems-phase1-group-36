# Stakeholder Analysis

The REMS system is designed to replace a fragmented and inefficient process currently used by the university. Based on the scenario, I identified stakeholders who are directly involved in the process, as well as those who are indirectly affected.

---

## 1. Researchers (Applicants)

### Role
Researchers are the ones who initiate the process. They prepare and submit ethics applications before starting any research involving human participants.

### Responsibilities
- Complete and submit ethics applications  
- Provide all required supporting documents (e.g., consent forms, risk assessments, information sheets)  
- Respond to feedback and revise applications  

### Value / Interest
Researchers want a process that is clear, fast, and transparent. In the current system, they face delays and confusion due to scattered documents and unclear communication. A unified system would help them track progress and reduce uncertainty.

### Researchers Significance
Researchers are the primary users of the system. Most core features (submission, document upload, tracking) are directly driven by their needs, so understanding their perspective is essential.

---

## 2. Administrative Staff

### Role
Administrative staff manage the overall flow of applications. They act as the bridge between researchers and the ethics committee.

### Responsibilities
- Check whether applications are complete  
- Track application status (currently using spreadsheets)  
- Assign applications to reviewers  
- Coordinate communication between stakeholders  

### Value / Interest
They need a system that reduces manual work and makes tracking easier. The current process is fragmented and difficult to manage, so a central system would significantly improve efficiency and reduce errors.

### Administrative Staff Significance
They are involved in almost every stage of the process. Their perspective helps us understand workflow issues, delays, and system coordination requirements.

---

## 3. Ethics Committee Members (Reviewers)

### Role
Committee members are responsible for reviewing applications and making decisions such as approval, rejection, or requesting revisions.

### Responsibilities
- Review applications and supporting documents  
- Provide feedback and recommendations  
- Participate in discussions with other reviewers  
- Declare conflicts of interest when necessary  

### Value / Interest
They need easy access to complete and up-to-date information. Currently, documents are scattered across different systems, making the review process inefficient. They also need support for managing conflicts of interest.

### Ethics Committee Members (Reviewers) Significance
Their role defines key parts of the system, including review workflows, decision-making, and ethical compliance. Without understanding their needs, the system cannot function effectively.

---

## 4. Study Participants (Indirect Stakeholder)

### Role
Study participants are individuals who take part in research studies approved through the system.

### Responsibilities
- Provide consent  
- Participate in research activities  

### Value / Interest
They are mainly concerned with privacy, safety, and ethical treatment. Even though they do not directly use the system, the system exists to protect their rights and wellbeing.

### Study Participants Significance
They represent the ethical purpose of the system. Their needs influence important requirements such as data confidentiality and risk management.

---


## Summary

These stakeholders were selected to cover different perspectives in the REMS process: submission (researchers), coordination (administrative staff), review (committee members), and ethical impact (participants). Together, they help us understand both functional and non-functional requirements of the system.
